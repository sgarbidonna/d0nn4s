Donna Sgarbi is an interdisciplinary artist specializing in 3D + AI technologies. Her work explores the creation of imaginary worlds where the fantastic and the real converge, giving rise to hybrid scenes that challenge the perception of the everyday.

Her background in painting and Scenography at the National University of La Plata (UNLP) shaped her interest in space as a narrative structure. In 2018, she expanded her practice into the digital field through studies in Computer Science at UNLP and intensive training in Blender, and since 2020 she has integrated AI as a core element of her creative process, exploring virtual and generative environments.

Her expertise in digital tools within the field of CGI encompasses the entire 3D and VFX pipeline—from pre to post-production—as well as the use of AI to optimize renders, develop controlled image generation through custom LoRA models, and produce video from generated imagery, among other ongoing lines of research.

At the core of her practice, Donna explores the intersection of 3D and AI from a contemporary surrealist perspective, using these technologies to construct alternative universes infused with realistic elements. Her work merges the oneiric, the symbolic, and the everyday into poetic, ambiguous spaces that invite an open, non-literal perceptual experience.
